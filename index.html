About Me

I am a first-year doctoral student at the Smart Materials Robotics Laboratory, Toyohashi University of Technology. My research areas include control of electro-ionic soft actuators (ionic polymer actuators), robotics using machine learning, and computer vision.

I worked full-time as an engineer at Kasaero GmbH (Germany) on fuel-cell and UAV systems and the development of a four-wheel robot. I also pursue personal projects, such as state estimation using temporal convolutional networks and ROS-based autonomous mobile robots.

My academic interests are in robotics, machine learning, and control systems. Recently, I have been focusing on applying autonomous robots to real-world problems.

Ongoing Research / Projects

Temperature Estimation of a Peltier Element Using TCN

Using a temporal convolutional network (TCN) to estimate the temperature response of a Peltier element in real time from current input. I collect experimental data and evaluate the model’s accuracy and response speed.

Autonomous Navigation of a Mecanum-Wheel Robot

Developing a ROS-based autonomous navigation system for a four-wheel mecanum robot. Integrates SLAM, path planning, and obstacle avoidance, building capabilities for future applied research.

Reinforcement Learning Control of a Two-Wheeled Inverted Pendulum

Applying reinforcement learning to balance and trajectory tracking of a two-wheeled inverted pendulum robot. I designed and built the robot from chassis to electronics, and compare Kalman filter + PID control with RL-based control.

Theses & Selected Results

Accelerating electro-ionic soft actuators by feedforward control and the performance degradation by voltage limits

Author: Junya Nakada
Type: International Conference Paper (SPIE, 2024)
Summary: Joint work with KAIST (South Korea) on faster electro-ionic soft actuators. We found that voltage saturation can degrade performance and clarified the conditions and extent of degradation via simulation and experiments.

Semantic segmentation and pose estimation for automated anchoring of formwork walls

Author: Junya Nakada
Type: Master's Thesis (University of Stuttgart, 2025)
Summary: Proposed a real-time pipeline combining YOLOv8-based semantic segmentation with pose estimation to detect and estimate the orientation/position of formwork anchor holes. Validated accuracy using IMU ground truth and analyzed error sources.

Acceleration of Electro-Ionic Soft Actuators by Feedforward Control under Voltage Constraints

Author: Junya Nakada
Type: Master's Thesis (Toyohashi University of Technology, 2025)
Summary: Explores acceleration of electro-ionic soft actuators while mitigating performance degradation due to voltage saturation by proposing a feedforward controller. Verified through simulations and experiments.

Implementation and Comparison of AI-based Image-Processing Methods for Concrete Hollow Sphere Detection

Author: Junya Nakada
Type: Student Thesis (University of Stuttgart, 2024)
Summary: Compared classical image processing (Hough Transform) with deep learning (YOLOv8) for detecting concrete hollow spheres in construction-like environments, demonstrating the robustness and accuracy of the learning-based approach.
