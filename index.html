About Me

I work at the intersection of robotics, computer vision, and control. My recent research focuses on perception and estimation methods that enable automation in real-world, safety-critical environments such as construction sites.

My master’s work at the University of Stuttgart (Institute for System Dynamics) studied real-time semantic segmentation and pose estimation for automated anchoring of formwork walls using YOLOv8-seg and planar pose estimation (solvePnP/IPPE), including evaluation with IMU-based ground truth.

In Japan, my master’s thesis focused on accelerating an electro-ionic soft actuator by designing a feedforward control method that remains effective under voltage saturation constraints, validated by simulation and experiments.

Research Topics

Construction Robotics & Vision-based Automation

Developing perception pipelines for construction automation: detecting task-relevant structures (e.g., anchor holes, concrete components) under challenging lighting, dust, and clutter. Emphasis on robustness and integration into real-time robotic workflows.

Pose Estimation from Segmentation/Detections

Estimating object pose from 2D detections and segmented regions using geometric methods (PnP / planar pose estimation). Investigating error factors such as camera-wall angle, perspective distortion, and bounding box deformation, and how these impact stability and accuracy.

Soft Robotics Control under Input Constraints

Control design for electro-ionic soft actuators with strict voltage limits. Designing feedforward approaches that reduce performance degradation from saturation and improve rise-time in experiments, with a focus on practical deployability.

Theses & Selected Results

Semantic segmentation and pose estimation for automated anchoring of formwork walls

Author: Junya Nakada
Type: Master’s Thesis, University of Stuttgart (2025)
Summary: Proposed a real-time pipeline combining YOLOv8-based semantic segmentation with pose estimation (solvePnP/IPPE) to detect and estimate the orientation/position of formwork anchor holes. Experiments validated accuracy using IMU ground truth, and analyzed failure modes caused by camera-wall angles and perspective-induced distortions.

Implementation and comparison of AI-based image-processing methods for concrete hollow spheres detection

Author: Junya Nakada
Type: Student Thesis, University of Stuttgart (2024)
Summary: Compared a classical image-processing approach (Hough Transform-based detection) with a deep-learning approach (YOLOv8) for detecting concrete hollow spheres in construction environments. Demonstrated stronger robustness/accuracy of the learning-based approach under site-like conditions.

Author: 中田 順也
